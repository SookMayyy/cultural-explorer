# LokaLearn — Mascot Design & Game Asset Resources

Where to design your mascot (Maya the Tapir / Rimau / Wak) and find attractive,
child-friendly game art. Grouped by what you need, with notes on what's free and
what to watch for.

---

## A. Designing or generating your mascot

**AI image generators (fastest for a custom mascot)**
- **Dreamina (CapCut)** — you already used this; great for cute illustrated
  characters and scenes. Good for backgrounds and mascot poses. Free tier.
- **Midjourney** — highest-quality illustrated style, paid. Excellent for a
  consistent, polished mascot if you can describe it well.
- **Microsoft Designer / Bing Image Creator** — free, DALL·E-based, decent for
  cartoon characters.
- **Leonardo.ai** — free tier, good for game art styles, has a "cartoon" / "anime"
  model selection.
- **Adobe Firefly** — free tier, commercially safer (trained on licensed data),
  good for a tapir mascot in a flat children's style.

> Tip for a *consistent* mascot: generate one good front-facing pose, then in your
> prompt ask for "the same character, same colours, in a waving pose / celebrating
> pose / pointing pose". Consistency across poses is the hard part — describe the
> colours and features precisely each time (e.g. "small purple tapir, round body,
> yellow scarf, big friendly eyes").

**Hand-drawing / vector tools (full control, no AI)**
- **Figma** — you already have this; good for simple flat vector mascots and all
  your UI. Free.
- **Inkscape** — free, open-source vector editor; export SVG/PNG.
- **Canva** — has a character/illustration library and easy editing; free tier.
- **Procreate** (iPad) — if you want to draw by hand; paid, one-time.

**Ready-made mascot/character art (if you don't want to design one)**
- **Flaticon** — huge library of cute animal icons/characters; free with attribution.
- **Freepik** — illustrated characters and scenes; free with attribution, paid for
  premium.
- **unDraw** — free flat illustrations (more for UI than mascots, but useful).
- **Storyset (by Freepik)** — free editable illustrated scenes, kid-friendly style.
- **OpenGameArt.org** — free game sprites and characters (check each licence).
- **Kenney.nl** — free, high-quality game asset packs (UI, characters, icons),
  public domain. Excellent for buttons, icons, and simple sprites.

## B. Backgrounds & scenes (the painted jungle/map look)

- **Dreamina / Midjourney / Leonardo** — best for the painted Malaysian
  kampung/jungle/map scenes you want behind the mascots.
- **Freepik / Vecteezy** — searchable illustrated backgrounds ("jungle cartoon
  background", "malaysia map illustration"); free with attribution.
- **Kenney.nl** — free flat background and tileset packs.

When you make these, export them at the sizes in your **ASSET_AND_SOUND_GUIDE.md**
(820×540 for full backgrounds) and drop them into the prototype's image slots.

---

## C. UI elements (buttons, badges, frames, icons)

- **Kenney.nl "UI Pack" / "Game Icons"** — free, public domain, perfect chunky
  kid-game buttons and panels.
- **Flaticon / Icons8** — icons for food, landmarks, stars, trophies; free with
  attribution.
- **Game-icons.net** — free SVG icons with a game feel (great for stamps/rewards).
- **Figma Community** — search "game UI kit" for free draggable component sets.

---

## D. Stamps, stickers & reward art

Your passport/stamp system needs small circular badge art:
- **Flaticon / Freepik** — search "badge", "stamp", "sticker" — lots of free
  circular designs you can recolour.
- **Canva** — has badge/sticker templates you can customise per state.
- Or generate them with AI: "circular passport stamp, gold border, cartoon tapir
  riding a bamboo horse, mangrove trees" — matches your written stamp descriptions
  exactly.

## E. Sound effects & music (for completeness)

- **bfxr.net** — free retro sound-effect generator (your correct/wrong/tap/reward
  sounds). Covered in your asset guide.
- **Freesound.org** — free sound effects (check licences; many are CC0).
- **Pixabay (audio)** — free music and effects, no attribution required.
- **Incompetech (Kevin MacLeod)** — free background music with attribution.

For voiceover narration (Maya reading the stories):
- **ElevenLabs** — free tier, very natural child-friendly voices; export MP3.
- **Browser Web Speech API** — free, built-in, no files needed (lower quality but
  zero cost and works offline).

## F. Licensing — important for your FYP

Whatever you use, **record the source and licence** for your report's appendix:
- "Free with attribution" → you must credit the creator (keep a credits list).
- "CC0 / public domain" (Kenney, some Freesound) → no attribution needed, safest.
- AI-generated → note which tool; check its terms allow your use (most free tiers
  allow educational/non-commercial use).

A short **"Assets & Credits"** table in your report shows academic integrity and
avoids any plagiarism concern. Example row:

| Asset | Source | Licence | Used for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tapir mascot | Dreamina (AI) | Free tier, educational | Maya character |
| Button UI | Kenney.nl | CC0 public domain | Game buttons |
| Correct sound | bfxr | Generated, free | Quiz feedback |

## Recommended starting set (to move fast)

If you want the shortest path: **Dreamina** for the mascot + backgrounds,
**Kenney.nl** for buttons/icons/UI, **Flaticon** for food/landmark images,
**bfxr** for sounds, **ElevenLabs** for Maya's voiceover. That covers everything
the focused game needs, almost all free.
